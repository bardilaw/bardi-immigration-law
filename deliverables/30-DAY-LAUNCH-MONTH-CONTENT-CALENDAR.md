### Day 10 — Wed June 19 · Myth-bust · IG + FB
**Notario fraud — "they said they could file it for me"**

```
MYTH: "A notario / immigration consultant can handle my case just like a lawyer."

TRUTH: In the U.S., a "notario público" is NOT an attorney — and only a licensed attorney (or an accredited representative) can give you legal advice on your immigration case.

This confusion costs families dearly. In many Latin American countries, a "notario" is a trained legal professional. In the U.S., the title means something completely different. Every year, people pay notarios who file the wrong forms, miss deadlines, or make promises no one can keep — and the person whose name is on the application pays the price.

How to protect yourself:
→ Ask if they're a licensed attorney or a DOJ-accredited representative.
→ Be cautious of anyone who "guarantees" results.
→ Get every fee and service in writing.

Your case is too important to gamble. Work with someone who's actually accountable to a bar association.

Questions? bardilaw.com — link in bio · Hablamos español
```

**TPS — Temporary Protected Status**

```
If your home country has been designated for Temporary Protected Status, you may be able to live and work in the U.S. legally — even if you don't have another immigration status.

TPS is granted to people from countries facing armed conflict, natural disaster, or other extraordinary conditions that make safe return difficult. It can provide:
✓ Protection from removal during the designation period
✓ Work authorization
✓ In some cases, permission to travel

A few important things:
→ TPS is country-specific and time-limited — designations and re-registration deadlines change, so staying current is critical.
→ TPS by itself is not a green card — but depending on your situation, it can fit into a larger strategy toward permanent status.
→ Missing a re-registration window can cost you your protection.

If you have TPS, are re-registering, or think you might qualify, it's worth reviewing your full picture with an attorney.
```
