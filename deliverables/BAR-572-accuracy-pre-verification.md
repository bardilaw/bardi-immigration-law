| Asylum protects those who fear harm based on **race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group** | ✅ | The five protected grounds, INA 101(a)(42) / 208. Exact. |

| **Affirmative asylum** — apply on your own with **USCIS**, **before** removal proceedings; interview with an **asylum officer** | ✅ | Exact (USCIS, The Affirmative Asylum Process). |
| **Defensive asylum** — requested as a defense **after** removal proceedings begin; heard by an **immigration judge** in court | ✅ | Exact (EOIR). |
| **One-year filing deadline** — generally must apply within **one year of last arrival** | ✅ | INA 208(a)(2)(B). Correct. |
| Exceptions: **changed circumstances** (e.g., country conditions) **or extraordinary personal circumstances** | 🟡 | Accurate. Optional precision: the regulatory terms are "changed circumstances" (materially affecting eligibility — incl. country conditions, changes in the applicant's circumstances, or changes in U.S. law) and "extraordinary circumstances" (events directly causing the late filing — e.g., serious illness, legal disability, ineffective counsel), per 8 CFR 208.4 / INA 208(a)(2)(D). The copy's plain-English version is faithful; no change required unless you want the term "extraordinary circumstances" verbatim. |

## Removal Defense page

| Claim in copy | Status | Source check |
|---|---|---|
| Removal proceeding = formal court process to deport; begins when **DHS files a Notice to Appear (NTA)** in immigration court | ✅ | Accurate (NTA = charging document, INA 239; filed with EOIR immigration court). |
| Missing a hearing can result in a **removal order in absentia** | ✅ | Correct (in absentia removal, INA 240(b)(5)). |
| Detained vs. non-detained representation; can move to **seek release** | ✅ | Accurate (bond / custody redetermination). |
| **Cancellation of removal** — "for certain **long-term residents** who meet strict **presence and hardship** requirements" | 🟡 | This is the line most worth your deliberate confirmation. It is not wrong, but it compresses **two different forms of relief** into one bullet, and "hardship" applies to only one of them: <br>• **42A (LPRs / INA 240A(a))**: 5+ yrs as an LPR, 7 yrs continuous residence after admission in any status, no aggravated felony. **No hardship showing required.** <br>• **42B (non-LPRs / INA 240A(b))**: 10 yrs continuous physical presence, good moral character, no disqualifying convictions, **and exceptional and extremely unusual hardship to a USC/LPR spouse, parent, or child.** <br>So "long-term residents… presence and hardship" actually describes the **42B (non-LPR)** path. For a high-level landing-page list this is acceptable, but please confirm you're comfortable that the single bullet covers both — or we can split it into two for precision. |
| Other defenses listed: Asylum, Adjustment of status, Bond hearings, Waivers and appeals incl. **Board of Immigration Appeals** | ✅ | All valid forms of relief / process; BIA is the correct appellate body. |
