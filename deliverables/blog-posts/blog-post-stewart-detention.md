## What Is Stewart Detention Center?

Stewart Detention Center is a federal immigration detention facility located in Lumpkin, Georgia — about 145 miles southwest of Atlanta. It is operated by **CoreCivic** under contract with U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and has been running since 2006.

With an official bed capacity of 1,752, Stewart is one of the **largest ICE detention facilities in the United States**. As of early 2026, it is operating significantly above capacity — housing over 2,000 detainees as Georgia has become one of the top five states nationally for federal immigration arrests.

One critical detail families often don't realize: Stewart has an **immigration court located on-site** — the Stewart Immigration Court (also called the Lumpkin Immigration Court). That means hearings can happen quickly, often within weeks of detention, not years. The detained docket moves fast. That urgency is real.

## The First 72 Hours: What Actually Happens

### Hour 1–24: Intake and Processing

After arrival at Stewart, your family member will go through ICE intake. This includes:

- Fingerprinting and identity verification
- Criminal history and immigration database checks
- Creation of a custody file
- Service of a **Notice to Appear (NTA)** — the formal document initiating removal proceedings
- An initial advisal of rights

During this period, ICE will make a **custody determination** — reviewing immigration history, criminal record, and ties to the community — to decide whether to offer bond and at what amount.

**Phone calls:** Detainees are entitled to make phone calls, including to family and an attorney. If you have not heard from your loved one, call the ICE detainee information line at **(888) 474-4744** or the ICE Detention Reporting and Information Line at **1-888-351-4024**. You can also call Stewart's main line directly at **229-838-5000**.

For automated case status, use EOIR's line: **1-800-898-7180**.

### Hour 24–72: Bond Determination and First Hearing Timeline

ICE will issue an initial custody decision — including whether bond is set and at what amount — within this window. Typical bond amounts at Stewart range from **$2,500 to $15,000**, with the Georgia median around $7,500.

If ICE denies bond or sets an amount the family cannot afford, the detainee can request a **bond redetermination hearing** before an immigration judge. These hearings are typically scheduled within one to two weeks of the request.

**Important 2026 update:** In May 2026, the U.S. Court of Appeals for the 11th Circuit struck down the Trump administration's mandatory detention policy in a 2–1 decision, ruling that ICE cannot deny bond hearings to people in immigration detention — including long-term U.S. residents with no criminal records. Bond hearings are currently available again for Stewart detainees. However, the government may appeal or seek a stay, so the situation is evolving. Contact an attorney immediately to understand the current status.

The **first master calendar hearing** — the initial immigration court appearance — is typically scheduled two to six weeks after detention.

## Your Rights While Detained

Your family member has rights, even in detention. These include:

- **The right to know why they are being detained**
- **The right to remain silent** — they do not have to answer questions from ICE or Border Patrol without an attorney present
- **The right to contact an attorney** — and to refuse to sign documents without legal advice
- **The right to phone calls** to family and legal counsel
- **The right to an interpreter** if needed

The most important thing to understand: **the U.S. government does not provide an attorney in immigration proceedings.** Unlike criminal court, there is no court-appointed counsel. If your family member does not have legal representation, they will face the immigration judge alone.

At Stewart, this is not a theoretical risk — it is the statistical reality. According to a report from the Southern Poverty Law Center, **as few as 6% of Stewart detainees have legal representation**. Legal aid organizations in the area have seen demand surge far beyond their capacity. Families who wait lose time they cannot get back.

## Why Speed Matters More at Stewart Than Anywhere Else

At Stewart, the immigration court is on-site. That means cases move faster than almost anywhere in the country. Non-detained immigration cases can take years to resolve; detained cases at Stewart can be decided in a matter of weeks.

If your loved one enters a hearing without an attorney, they may not know:

- How to request a continuance to find representation
- How to request a bond reduction
- Whether they qualify for asylum, cancellation of removal, or other relief
- How to preserve their rights for any future appeal

Every week without an attorney is a week the government is preparing its case. Detained dockets do not pause.
