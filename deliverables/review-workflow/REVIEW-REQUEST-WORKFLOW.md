## Before You Use This: 3 Rules That Never Bend

These come from Georgia Bar Rule 7.3 and the Alabama Rules of Professional Conduct 7.3. Carried forward from [BAR-48](/BAR/issues/BAR-48).

1. **Ask only at a natural endpoint.** Request a review after a *concluded* matter or a clearly positive milestone (approval granted, case closed, petition filed and receipted). Do not ask a client who has anxious decisions still pending — it reads as pressure.
2. **Never offer anything of value** in exchange for a review. No discounts, no fee credits, no gifts. The ask is free, or it is a bar violation.
3. **Never write or edit the review.** If a client asks "what should I say?", point them to the prompts in the SOP — never hand them text to paste.

## 1. Review Request Email Template

Short, personal, milestone-triggered. Send **after** a warm human moment (the call or email delivering good news), not cold.

### Subject line — A/B test these

- **A (warm/personal):** A quick favor, [FIRST_NAME]
- **B (specific/contextual):** Would you share your experience with Bardi Immigration Law?

> Run A for the first ~15 sends, B for the next ~15, and keep whichever earns more clicks-to-review. Track in the GBP insights tab (see SOP §4).

> Hi [FIRST_NAME],
>

## 3. Review Request SOP (One Page)

### When to ask
- **Trigger on a milestone, not the calendar:** case approval, case closure, a filed-and-receipted petition, or a positive consultation follow-up where the client declined to proceed but was happy with the guidance.
- **Best window:** within **3–5 days** of the good news, while the relief is fresh. Waiting two weeks halves response rates.
- **Ask once per matter.** Do not re-ask the same client for the same case.

### How to ask — channel sequence
1. **Day 0 — Email.** Send the §1 template (Spanish version for non-English-preferring clients) right after the milestone moment.
2. **Day 3 — Text, if no review yet.** Send the §2 SMS script *only* if the client texts with the firm and hasn't reviewed.
3. **Stop after two touches.** If there's no review by ~day 7, let it go. A third ask damages the relationship and risks looking transactional — off-brand for a boutique firm.

### What to do with negative or private feedback
- If a client replies unhappy **instead of** posting publicly, treat it as a gift: call them, listen, and offer to make it right. Resolving it privately is the win — do not steer them to post.
- If a 1–2 star review **does** land, respond publicly per §4 (calm, professional, invite offline), then resolve offline. Never argue facts or reference case specifics in public — confidentiality applies.
- Never ask Google to remove a review unless it violates Google policy (spam, off-topic, not a real client). Disputing honest criticism backfires.

### Where to track
- **Monthly:** review the **GBP Insights** tab — total reviews, average rating, new reviews this month, and which subject-line variant (§1 A/B) drove them.
- **Targets** (from `VISION.md`): 4.5+ stars and 10+ reviews by ~Feb 2027 (9-month mark); 15 reviews within 90 days of launch; 30 within 6 months.
- Log each send in a simple sheet (client first name, matter, channel, date asked, reviewed Y/N) so you never double-ask and can see conversion.

## 4. Review Response Templates

Respond to **every** review within 48 hours — Google rewards engagement and prospects read the replies as closely as the reviews. Keep replies short, warm, and confidentiality-safe. **Never confirm someone was a client of a specific case type or mention any case detail in a public reply.**

### 5-star positive — Version 1 (warm)

> Thank you so much for your kind words. It was a genuine privilege to walk alongside you, and trust like yours is exactly why we do this work. Wishing you and your family all the best. — Eszter

### 5-star positive — Version 2 (forward-looking)

> This means a great deal to our small team — thank you for taking the time to share it. We're grateful you trusted Bardi Immigration Law, and we're always here if you ever need us again. — Eszter

### 3–4 star mixed

> Thank you for the honest feedback — we take it seriously. We're always working to make a stressful process feel clearer, and your input helps us do that better. If there's anything we can still make right, please reach out to me directly at [PHONE]. — Eszter

### 1–2 star negative (de-escalate, invite offline)

> I'm sorry your experience didn't meet the standard we hold ourselves to. I take this seriously and would welcome the chance to understand what happened and make it right — please contact me directly at [PHONE] or [EMAIL]. — Eszter

> **Negative-reply rules:** Stay calm, never defensive. Do not confirm or deny the relationship, dispute facts, or reference case details — confidentiality applies even when the reviewer waived it. The goal of a public reply is to show *future* readers that the firm responds professionally; the actual resolution happens offline.

### Spanish reply (use when the review is in Spanish)

> Muchas gracias por sus amables palabras. Fue un verdadero privilegio acompañarle, y la confianza como la suya es exactamente por lo que hacemos este trabajo. Le deseamos a usted y a su familia lo mejor. — Eszter
