## 2. Services Offered

### Benefits-Based Immigration
- Naturalization (citizenship applications)
- Lawful Permanent Resident (LPR) status / green cards
- Family-based petitions (I-130)
- Temporary Protected Status (TPS)
- Waivers: I-601, I-212, I-929
- Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)
- Special Immigrant Juvenile Status (SIJS)
- Violence Against Women Act (VAWA) petitions
- U-visa (victims of violent crime)

### Removal Defense
- Detained and non-detained removal defense
- Bond hearings
- Cancellation of Removal (42B non-LPR and 42A LPR)

### Federal Litigation
- Habeas corpus petitions
- Mandamus petitions

### NOT Currently Offered (but flagged for future)
- Personal injury (client expressed interest in adding this later)

## 3. Ideal Customer Profiles

### ICP 1: Benefits-Based (Primary Volume Driver)
- **Age:** Millennial or Gen-Z
- **Background:** In the US since childhood; seeking to normalize status through marriage or humanitarian pathways (U-visa, VAWA)
- **Also:** Individuals with a US citizen or green card holder parent who previously filed a petition
- **Industry:** No preference; many current clients in construction or service industries
- **Income:** $30,000+/year
- **Location:** Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina (primary)
- **Platform behavior:** Active on social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, Google Search)

### ICP 2: Humanitarian / Juvenile
- **Age:** Under 18
- **Background:** Unaccompanied minors in the US without a parent
- **Acquisition:** Word of mouth (not social media)
- **Services:** SIJS, shepherding through full immigration journey

### ICP 3: Removal Defense
- **Age:** Any
- **Background:** Resided in US for several years; at risk of deportation
- **Industry:** Construction, service sector
- **Income:** $30,000+
- **Location:** Georgia, North Carolina, South Carolina
- **Urgency:** High — facing removal proceedings, detention, or NTA

## 4. Core Value Proposition

**Problem solved:** Complex immigration problems. Without resolution, clients face deportation, lack of work permits, inability to get a driver's license.

**Differentiators (client's own words):**
1. Creative, competent, and complex legal representation
2. Treats every client as family — personally invested in outcomes
3. 2025-26 Chapter Chair, Georgia-Alabama AILA — well-connected nationally, well-informed on legal changes
4. Direct attorney communication (not delegated to paralegals or intake specialists)

**Positioning:** Boutique practice. Not a factory. Client interaction starts with an attorney consultation, not an intake specialist.
