# Bardi Immigration Law — Competitive UI & Design Analysis

> Client deliverable. Accompanies `COMPETITIVE-LANDSCAPE.md` (market positioning) with visual/UX analysis of all five client-named competitors. Includes site structure maps, look-and-feel breakdowns, and design recommendations for Bardi's website.
>
> Last updated: 2026-05-19

---

## Executive Summary

We analyzed the websites of all five competitors you identified — Tadeo & Silva, Manji Law, Diaz & Gaeta, Antonini & Cohen, and Vazquez & Servi — focusing on visual design, site structure, user experience, and conversion strategy. The findings confirm a clear design opportunity for Bardi: **no firm in your competitive set owns a minimalist, boutique aesthetic.** Every competitor either leans corporate-heavy (Antonini & Cohen, Diaz & Gaeta), warm-but-generic (Tadeo & Silva), content-dense (Manji Law), or neglected (Vazquez & Servi). Bardi can differentiate on design clarity alone.

This document covers:
1. Per-competitor visual breakdown (site map, UI patterns, color palette, strengths/weaknesses)
2. Cross-competitor comparison matrix
3. Bardi recommended site outline
4. Bardi layout and look-and-feel recommendations

## Part 3: Recommended Site Outline for Bardi Immigration Law

Based on: competitive analysis + CLIENT-BRIEF.md + BRAND-IDENTITY.md + client's minimalist/boutique direction.

### Design Principles (Derived from Competitive Gaps)

1. **Own the minimalist lane.** No competitor does this. Warm Gray backgrounds, generous whitespace, restrained content.
2. **Attorney-first, not intake-first.** Every section reinforces direct attorney access.
3. **Quality over volume.** 2 great blog posts/month beats Antonini's 5 content streams.
4. **Southeast reach, not just Atlanta.** Location pages for GA + AL + NC + SC capitals — not 15 Atlanta suburbs.
5. **Published fees.** No competitor does this. Massive trust differentiator.
6. **Bilingual parity.** Match Tadeo & Silva's full Spanish translation.

### Recommended Information Architecture

```
Home
├── Practice Areas
│   ├── Benefits-Based Immigration
│   │   ├── Naturalization & Citizenship
│   │   ├── Green Cards & Adjustment of Status
│   │   ├── Family-Based Petitions (I-130)
│   │   ├── TPS
│   │   ├── DACA
│   │   └── Waivers (I-601, I-212, I-929)
│   ├── Humanitarian Immigration
│   │   ├── VAWA
│   │   ├── U-Visa
│   │   └── Special Immigrant Juvenile Status (SIJS)
│   ├── Removal Defense
│   │   ├── Detained & Non-Detained Defense
│   │   ├── Bond Hearings
│   │   └── Cancellation of Removal
│   └── Federal Litigation
│       ├── Habeas Corpus Petitions
│       └── Mandamus Petitions
├── About
│   ├── Meet Attorney Bardi (founder story + credentials)
│   └── AILA Leadership (2025-26 Chapter Chair, GA-AL)
├── Fees (flat-fee schedule — unique differentiator)
├── Locations
│   ├── Georgia (Atlanta, primary)
│   ├── Alabama (Birmingham, Huntsville, Mobile)
│   ├── North Carolina (Charlotte, Raleigh)
│   └── South Carolina (Columbia, Greenville)
├── Blog (2 substantive posts/month)
├── Contact / Book a Consultation
├── [Español — full site translation]
└── Footer
    ├── Practice areas links
    ├── Contact info
    ├── Social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube)
    └── AILA badge + credentials
```

**Estimated page count at launch:** ~25-30 pages

**Why this structure works:**

- **Shallower than Manji/Antonini** — matches boutique positioning. Deep enough for SEO, lean enough for clarity.
- **Federal Litigation as top-level category** — no competitor surfaces this prominently. Signals capability most boutiques bury.
- **Fees page** — zero competitors publish fees. This page will rank for "immigration lawyer cost Atlanta" and build trust.
- **7 location pages** (vs. Manji's 15+) — targets Southeast quadrant capitals, not Atlanta suburb SEO stuffing.
- **About page structured around AILA Chair credential** — matches Antonini's "Super Lawyers" strategy with a harder-to-copy credential.
- **No multi-stream content** — just one blog. Counter to Antonini's 5-stream approach. Better for a solo practice.

| Order | Section | Content | Design Pattern | Competitive Reference |

| 1 | **Nav** | Logo (horizontal full-color) + "Book a Consultation" CTA + nav links + Español toggle | Sticky, minimal; Navy text on Warm Gray | Simpler than all 5 competitors |
| 2 | **Hero** | "Your Attorney, From Day One." + subhead about direct attorney access + single CTA (Book a Consultation) | Full-width, Warm Gray bg, Navy text, Gold CTA button, no carousel | Counter to Tadeo & Silva's carousel and Diaz & Gaeta's form |
| 3 | **Credential Bar** | AILA GA-AL Chapter Chair 2025-26 badge + "Immigration Law Exclusively" + Multi-State (GA/AL/NC/SC) | Single horizontal strip, restrained | Like Antonini's trust signals but minimal |
| 4 | **Services** | 4 practice area cards (Benefits / Humanitarian / Removal Defense / Federal Litigation) | White cards on Warm Gray, Navy headings, Gold icon accents | Cleaner than Tadeo & Silva's cards |
