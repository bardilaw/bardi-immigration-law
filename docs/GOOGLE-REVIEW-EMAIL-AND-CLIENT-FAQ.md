### Frequently Asked Questions

---

**1. What happens after I contact Bardi Immigration Law?**

When you reach out, you'll schedule a consultation directly with Attorney Eszter Bardi — not an intake specialist or paralegal. During the consultation, we'll review your situation, explain your options, and give you a clear picture of what the process looks like before you commit to anything. There's no obligation from an initial call.

---

**2. Do you offer consultations in Spanish?**

Yes. Bardi Immigration Law serves clients in both English and Spanish. You are welcome to communicate in the language you're most comfortable with throughout your case — from the first consultation through the final resolution.

---

**3. Can I work with you if I don't live near your office?**

Yes. Bardi Immigration Law represents clients across Georgia, Alabama, North Carolina, and South Carolina. Consultations and ongoing case communication can be conducted remotely by phone or video — you do not need to be in the same city to work with us.

---

**4. How much does it cost to work with you?**

Fees vary depending on the type of case and the complexity of your situation. We'll provide a clear fee estimate at your initial consultation before you decide to move forward. Government filing fees (paid directly to USCIS or immigration courts) are separate and vary by case type — we'll explain those as part of the cost conversation. We do not charge a consultation fee.

---

**5. How long will my case take?**

Processing times depend on the type of benefit or proceeding, the court or agency involved, and your individual circumstances. Immigration processes often take months to years — we'll give you a realistic timeline at your consultation based on current processing data. We'll keep you informed at every stage and will never overestimate outcomes to win your business.
