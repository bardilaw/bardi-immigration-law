## PRACTICE AREAS — DACA

### Page Header

**H1:** DACA — Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals

**Intro:** DACA provides temporary protection from deportation and work authorization for individuals who came to the United States as children. If your status is approaching expiration — or if you've never applied — we can help you understand your options.

---

### What Is DACA?

**H2:** What DACA Does

**Body:** DACA grants a renewable period of deferred action — a temporary pause in removal proceedings — along with work authorization. It does not provide a green card or a path to citizenship on its own, but it protects recipients from deportation and allows them to work legally while federal policy continues to evolve.

---

### Who Qualifies?

**H2:** Eligibility Requirements

**Body:** To qualify for DACA, you must meet specific continuous residence, age, education, and criminal history criteria. Requirements have shifted over time due to ongoing federal litigation. Attorney Bardi reviews your complete record and advises you on whether you are currently eligible to apply or renew.

Don't guess about your eligibility. The rules are technical and the stakes are real.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** Attorney Bardi reviews your individual history before filing anything — identifying travel history, prior encounters with law enforcement, or gaps in continuous presence that could affect your case. She prepares your application completely and accurately, and she prepares you for any additional steps USCIS may require.

## PRACTICE AREAS — SIJS

### Page Header

**H1:** Special Immigrant Juvenile Status (SIJS)

**Intro:** SIJS provides a path to a green card for children in the United States who have been abused, neglected, or abandoned and cannot safely be reunited with one or both parents. This is specialized, sensitive legal work — and we handle it with the care it requires.

---

### What Is SIJS?

**H2:** What SIJS Provides

**Body:** Special Immigrant Juvenile Status is a federal immigration classification for eligible minors. A successful SIJS case involves two steps: first, obtaining findings from a state juvenile or family court; second, using those findings to petition for an immigrant visa and, ultimately, a green card. When both steps succeed, the child can obtain lawful permanent resident status in the United States.

---

### Who Qualifies?

**H2:** Eligibility

**Body:** SIJS is available to unmarried individuals under 21 who are in the United States and meet specific requirements related to abuse, neglect, or abandonment — and for whom a return to their home country would not be in their best interest. Eligibility is fact-specific and requires both a state court process and federal immigration filings.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** SIJS cases require navigating both state court and federal immigration systems simultaneously. Attorney Bardi coordinates the legal work across both, ensuring the required judicial findings are obtained and that the federal petition is prepared correctly and filed in time.

This work is sensitive. We approach it with the care the children and families we serve deserve.

## PRACTICE AREAS — VAWA

### Page Header

**H1:** VAWA — Violence Against Women Act Petitions

**Intro:** If you have experienced abuse from a U.S. citizen or permanent resident spouse, parent, or adult child, you may be able to petition for immigration relief on your own — without the abuser's knowledge. This process is confidential.

---

### What Is VAWA?

**H2:** Your Right to Self-Petition

**Body:** The Violence Against Women Act allows certain abuse survivors to self-petition for immigration status without depending on — or alerting — the abuser. USCIS does not contact the abusive family member when a VAWA petition is filed.

You do not need a police report. You do not need a prior protective order. You do not need to have pressed charges.

---

### Who Qualifies?

**H2:** Eligibility

**Body:** You may qualify to file a VAWA self-petition if you have experienced battery or extreme cruelty from a U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident who is your current or former spouse, parent, or adult child. You must have lived with the abuser at some point and be a person of good moral character.

VAWA protection extends to spouses, children, and parents — regardless of gender.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** Attorney Bardi handles VAWA petitions with complete confidentiality and sensitivity. She helps you gather the documentation required without putting you at risk, and prepares a personal declaration that accurately and completely presents your situation to USCIS.

If you are in a dangerous situation right now, your safety comes first. We can discuss your legal options in a way that does not create risk for you.

## PRACTICE AREAS — U-VISA

### Page Header

**H1:** U-Visa — Protection for Victims of Crime

**Intro:** If you were the victim of a violent crime in the United States and cooperated — or are willing to cooperate — with law enforcement, you may qualify for a U-visa. A U-visa provides legal status and work authorization, with a path to a green card after three years.

---

### What Is the U-Visa?

**H2:** What the U-Visa Provides

**Body:** The U-visa is a nonimmigrant status created specifically for crime victims who assist law enforcement. It provides up to four years of legal status and work authorization. After three years of continuous U-visa status, eligible holders can apply for a green card.

The U-visa is available regardless of current immigration status.

---

### Who Qualifies?

**H2:** Eligibility

**Body:** To qualify, you must have been a victim of a qualifying crime, suffered substantial physical or mental abuse, have information about the crime, and be willing to cooperate with law enforcement. A law enforcement certification — signed by a qualifying official — is required as part of the application.

Qualifying crimes include domestic violence, sexual assault, kidnapping, trafficking, robbery, and many others.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** Attorney Bardi guides you through the certification process, working with law enforcement on your behalf to obtain the required documentation. She then prepares and files the complete U-visa petition.

All consultations on U-visa matters are fully confidential.

## PRACTICE AREAS — TPS

### Page Header

**H1:** Temporary Protected Status (TPS)

**Intro:** If you are from a country designated for Temporary Protected Status, you may be eligible for protection from removal and work authorization while that designation is in effect. TPS registration deadlines are strict — don't miss yours.

---

### What Is TPS?

**H2:** What TPS Provides

**Body:** Temporary Protected Status is a program that allows nationals of certain countries to live and work legally in the United States on a temporary basis. Countries are designated for TPS by the Secretary of Homeland Security when they are experiencing ongoing armed conflict, environmental disaster, or other extraordinary conditions that make return unsafe.

TPS does not create a direct path to a green card, but it prevents removal and authorizes work during the designation period.

---

### Who Qualifies?

**H2:** Eligibility

**Body:** TPS eligibility depends on your country of origin and the specific registration dates for your country's designation. You must have been continuously present in the United States since a specified date and meet other requirements. Prior criminal convictions can disqualify you.

TPS designations change. Attorney Bardi monitors these changes through her AILA role and advises clients on current eligibility and deadlines.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** TPS registrations are time-sensitive. Attorney Bardi prepares your initial or re-registration application, identifies any issues in your record that need to be addressed, and evaluates whether TPS connects to any other forms of relief available in your situation.

## PRACTICE AREAS — FAMILY-BASED IMMIGRATION

### Page Header

**H1:** Family-Based Immigration

**Intro:** U.S. citizens and permanent residents can petition to bring certain family members to the United States. The process is complex — processing times, visa categories, and eligibility rules vary significantly. We help you navigate it correctly from the start.

---

### Who Can Be Sponsored?

**H2:** Eligible Family Relationships

**Body:**

**If you are a U.S. citizen, you can petition for:**
- Your spouse
- Your unmarried children under 21
- Your parents
- Your adult married or unmarried children
- Your siblings

**If you are a Lawful Permanent Resident (green card holder), you can petition for:**
- Your spouse
- Your unmarried children

Processing times vary significantly by category and your family member's country of origin.

**H2:** When It Gets Complicated

**Body:** Family-based immigration isn't always straightforward. Your family member may have prior periods of unlawful presence, prior immigration violations, or a prior order of removal. These issues do not always bar immigration relief — but they require careful legal strategy and, often, a waiver filing alongside the primary petition.

Attorney Bardi identifies these issues early and builds a plan that accounts for them.

## PRACTICE AREAS — WAIVERS

### Page Header

**H1:** Immigration Waivers (I-601, I-212, I-929)

**Intro:** Certain immigration bars can be waived — but only with a strong, well-documented showing. If you've been told you are inadmissible, a waiver may be the path forward.

---

### What Are Immigration Waivers?

**H2:** When You Need a Waiver

**Body:** Some individuals are inadmissible to the United States — meaning they cannot receive a visa or green card — due to prior unlawful presence, a prior removal, or other grounds. In many cases, these bars can be waived if the applicant qualifies and presents compelling evidence of hardship to a qualifying U.S. citizen or permanent resident family member.

Common waivers include:
- **I-601** — Unlawful presence (3-year or 10-year bar)
- **I-212** — Prior removal or deportation bar
- **I-929** — Derivative VAWA-related waiver

---

### The Hardship Standard

**H2:** What Waivers Require

**Body:** Waiver cases turn on whether the denial of the waiver would cause "extreme hardship" to a qualifying U.S. citizen or permanent resident family member. This is a high bar — ordinary hardship doesn't meet it. Attorney Bardi builds the full legal and factual record: evidence of financial, medical, educational, and emotional hardship to your qualifying relative, combined with a legal brief that frames the evidence in the way USCIS adjudicators are trained to evaluate.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** Attorney Bardi evaluates your eligibility, identifies your qualifying relative, and builds the evidence package that gives your waiver petition the best possible chance. She has experience preparing these complex filings and understanding what moves adjudicators.

**Intro:** If you've received a Notice to Appear or are in immigration detention, time is critical. Bardi Immigration Law represents clients in both detained and non-detained removal proceedings — from bond hearings through trial and appeal.

### What Is Removal Defense?

**H2:** What Removal Proceedings Are

**Body:** Removal proceedings begin when the Department of Homeland Security files a Notice to Appear (NTA) in immigration court, initiating a case to deport you from the United States. Removal cases can be detained — you are held in immigration custody — or non-detained, where you are free to remain in the community while your case proceeds.

Removal cases are serious. The consequences of losing are permanent.

## PRACTICE AREAS — FEDERAL LITIGATION

### Page Header

**H1:** Federal Immigration Litigation

**Intro:** When administrative options are exhausted — or when federal agencies act unlawfully — federal court may be the answer. We file habeas corpus and mandamus petitions in U.S. District Court.

---

### What Is Federal Immigration Litigation?

**H2:** When Federal Court Applies

**Body:** Federal immigration litigation involves filing suit in a U.S. District Court to challenge government action or compel government inaction. Unlike immigration court, federal district courts can review certain agency decisions, address constitutional claims, and order relief that the immigration system itself cannot provide.

Two common types:
- **Habeas corpus** — challenging the legality of immigration detention when a person is being held unlawfully or for an unreasonable period
- **Mandamus** — compelling USCIS or another agency to act on an application that has been pending for an unreasonably long time

---

### Is Federal Litigation Right for Your Case?

**H2:** Who Should Consider Federal Litigation

**Body:** Federal litigation may be appropriate if:
- Your USCIS application has been pending far beyond normal processing times with no explanation
- You or a family member has been detained beyond what the law allows and bond has been denied or was unreasonably set
- You've exhausted administrative appeals and a federal forum is the remaining option
- Your case involves a constitutional claim that immigration courts cannot resolve

Not every delayed case warrants federal litigation. Attorney Bardi evaluates whether the facts and timeline support a viable federal claim.

---

### How We Help

**H2:** What Attorney Bardi Does

**Body:** Attorney Bardi prepares and files the federal petition, represents you in district court proceedings, and litigates the case through resolution. Federal immigration cases require a different skill set than administrative immigration practice — not every immigration attorney handles them. This is work we do.
